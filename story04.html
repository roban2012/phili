老太太旅游游准备

老妈60多岁了，不会英语也没出过国（港澳台不算）。经过一番怂恿，老太太决心一搏。

我们在越南和菲律宾之间选择，5天游费用都在3000人民币左右。最后，还是选了“南美洲天主教资本主义海岛”菲律宾。我妈一个人独闯龙潭，只要精彩，不敢惊险。对比计划和路书，给家里父母出行提供一个参考

老年人都爱节俭，要说服他们一定要说明团费相当划算。要省钱又要确保安全，最好选大旅行社（批量大名声好）。在广州，有广之旅和南湖国旅，价格便宜量又足，很少纠纷事故。

要寻得低价，首先要货比三家，时不时询价。一旦有想去的地方，不妨路过门店时问一下。广之旅比较正规，网上都有。但南湖国旅的报价变化较大，网上没有更新。8月19日，南湖国旅有9月2日的宿务（宿雾、Cebu）1999元人民币，加300元薄荷岛一晚4星海滨酒店。 经过不断宣传动员，我妈在咨询各方意见后，于8月21日决定要去。带着护照跑到南湖国旅门店，发现提价到2199人民币了。看不懂，不做。路过另外一家店，还是一样。以前从来没有走过南湖国旅，还是暂时作罢。老太太决定不去了，还是害怕。

更多资讯，请访问： retter2012.com 。

8月23日跟南湖国旅去海边玩了一下，还不错。在网上查到广之旅有越南团699人民币的，加其他费用差不多2000人民币，还有北海银滩。不错。8月26日计划去广之旅报名。路过南湖国旅，顺便进去问问，有9月8日2199人民币宿雾薄荷5天的团。出来跟老妈商量，决定去之。在我不断承诺把自己在菲律宾的经验写出来给她之后，直接交钱下单。我说不用买保险，但老妈在订单写好后又加买80元太平洋的保险。实际上，我们没有拿到保险单，出发前给领队说了也没用。

还好没去越南。我们9月13日去了广西北海（有传说很不错的银滩），坐车9小时。银滩水质很差到处是死鱼，能见度不到20厘米。沙滩很长没有浪，但有救生员的不到200米。表面3厘米的沙很好，下面的沙是黑色的。沙地下有很多螺和蟹，他们负责吃掉死鱼。如此估计，越南北部恐怕已经遭了殃。北海银滩安全管理简单粗暴，冲淡水要25元。2013年9月14日16时，下了点小雨就把游客赶上岸（用摩托艇）。摩托艇驱赶过程中，还意外撞伤一东北游客。我还没开始游就结束了，真扫兴。中国境内哪有美景可寻？

宿雾薄荷5天的团包括薄荷到一晚高级沙滩酒店，1天自由行没有购物点，很爽。老婆上次没去成说要跟丈母娘再去一次，这次可以弥补一下。麦哲伦（Magellan）都去过Cebu，老太太也想看看。

定下9月8日去菲律宾宿雾后，老妈就开始准备了。好在身体还行，人也比较外向。没什么好担心的。老妈不敢浮潜（Snorkeling），根据旅行社行程安排，商量好出行计划（9月5日定稿）。9月6日早上，老妈突然决定要在薄荷沙滩酒店的游泳池玩水。于是，我立即推荐尝试一下浮潜（Snorkeling）。来太太半推半就默许。我立刻搞到一套泳装，趁热打铁，下午去练习一下。最后，我们发现准备工作非常有帮助。

9月6日，带老妈去越秀泳场练习浮潜（Snorkeling），老太太不会游泳，在水里折腾了1个小时，嘴唇发紫，提前上岸。后来在更衣室还吐了。于是，老妈决定不去浮潜（Snorkeling）。

老太太建议自己带一个水杯，可以接饮用水喝。省了卖水的钱。注意，只有中国人喝热水。国外都是冷水或冰水的。

9月7日，领队打电话，问清楚吃海鲜是在无忧岛，保险的事情他也应承下来。8月30日顺德一个泰国团飞机出事，还己任受伤扯皮，一直没解决。查看天气，没有台风，天天都有Tstorm，雷阵雨。

9月8日（周日）晚8点，老妈坐地铁去广州白云机场集合出发。回程9月12晚9点半，宿雾飞广州。 菲航的登机牌，除了名字拼音，老妈都不认识。



此行目的是了解拉丁美洲国家（菲律宾）风土人情。

跟团旅游，最好结伴而行。一个人走，可以找人搭伙，分担路费，有个照应还好点餐。所有计划都是计划，具体要因地制宜随机应变。无论如何，有个计划心里有底，不会慌张。老妈普通退休工人一个，好在身体不错，心还未老。背包（backpacking）就不要要求太多啦。

第1天的路书（9月8日）

9月8日23:50过安检、边检；0:40登机，1:20起飞。

领队开会，180多人的团，开心兴奋。老妈检查了机票、签证，了解注意事项。

跟领队商量自由行部分，推到地接导游那里。老妈感觉自己很开心，其他就懒得管了。也没问保险单的事。找到了小玲作伴，开心中。

这架飞机是旅游包机，大部分是南湖的客人。其中，广州白云区的很多，看来是纯粹玩乐的朋友圈。出境的边检事先安排好的，没问题。

9月9日凌晨4:10到达宿雾（Cebu）的麦克坦机场。下雨，工作人员打伞来接。中国人从来没有收到过这种待遇，感动。

9月9日凌晨，4:40办完入境手续，全团人都很顺利。老妈按计划在机场换汇，美元兑比索比率竟然是42.25。后来有人在SM换到44的！当真是菲律宾比索贬值了。

第2天的路书（9月9日）

9月9日6点坐车到宿雾的小旅馆。有一个车是中旅的，其他都是南湖的，人多，分5个车。早餐是粥、面包、西瓜、菠萝和炒鸡蛋。每天都吃炒鸡蛋。房间有空调，但是手动开关，老妈不懂，请领队帮忙才行。菲律宾的导游叫史派克，姓蔡，华人。助游叫罗密欧，当地人。老妈跟湖南长沙来的小玲结伴同游。小玲是干部，人很好。

阿蔡介绍旅店，美国人开的，时间较久了。中途推荐大家买水，大家去抢水。有人错卖了饮料，120多比索一瓶2升。1.5升的水都卖光了。老妈觉得被忽悠了。

车一启动，阿蔡就开始推销菲律宾的土特产。首先是椰子油，先让游客试用。老太太发现有免费的，就不再擦自己带的50倍欧莱雅了。然后是7D芒果干（170比索100克）、香蕉干和海绵（2500比索）。

宿雾（Cebu）背包地图。菲律宾英语带西班牙口音，街道又是西班牙语，最好找目的地忽略街道门牌。

这一天看圣婴教堂、十字架。圣婴教堂人很多，排队。很热，没敢走远。老太太对这些没什么印象。

晚饭后到SM，SM超大，豪华。找屈臣氏花了很长时间，但没有蛇粉。SM买的200克芒果干纸盒装，114比索，没有小包装。后来在Caisano超市，100克芒果干48比索。阿蔡说Cebu的出口到欧洲，国内只有7D的。我们吃过Cebu和7D，还是Cebu好吃些。阿蔡说，7D是用成熟芒果做的，Cebu用的青芒果。

SM内Max's吃了烤鱼，但是油炸的，不好吃。吃了炒冰（Mango Shake），芒果味好吃。妈和小玲后来迷路了，上车差点迟到。上车后还没人迷失其中。然后，回旅馆。其实，SM距离旅馆很近。

导游不允许自己走，妈又禁不住导游忽悠，说不安全，打不到车。加上另外两个团友放弃了自己走。老妈只好去看海豚，交400人民币。她只参加一次自费活动。其实，看海豚、上海岛、浮潜三项收400人民币还是划算的。

深度(5 - 40 m)

能见度15-30M

Top 10 best dive sites in the World

1. SS Yongala Wreck

The SS Yongala Wreck might just be THE Best Wreck dive in the World! This 110m long steamer called the Yongala was sunk in 1911 near Townsville during a cyclone. It was only re-discovered in 1958 and is now the most famous dive site in Australia. It is home to an amazing array of marine life which includes barracudas, Giant Trevallies Turtles and Sea Snakes. You might even spot the rare Bull Shark and the mighty Tiger Shark!!

澳大利亚东北部的大堡礁

大堡礁延绵2000公里，由2000个独立礁石和71个珊瑚岛组成的大堡礁大概是世界上最著名的潜水胜地了。这里有数不清的潜水机会，其中有著名的鳕鱼洞, Yongala船骸和Pixie Pinnacle。在这里，住在船上是最佳的潜水方式。

SS Yongala原本是英国的一艘钢铁客运和货运轮船，建于1903年，造价102000英镑。1911年3月23日，Yongala遭遇风暴，无声息地沉默在澳大利亚南部的汤斯维尔，直到1958年才被发现。现在的Yongala有大量海洋生物。每年造访这艘沉船的潜水者逾万人。110米长的身躯让它成为世界上最大的历史性沉船。

潜水类型：沉船潜水

到达方式：从澳大利亚北昆士兰汤斯维尔(Townsville) (约3小时)或者Ayr出发(约30分钟)出发，乘船可达。

能见度：10米到12米

SS扬加拉号全长110米，于1911年遭遇一场风暴而沉至海底，从此沉没在海底长达半个世纪。它是世界上最大、最完整的失事船之一，也是世界上最好的潜水沉船之一。这座沉船藏身于28米以下的水底，周身被珊瑚覆盖，拥有难以置信的海洋生物阵容。在晴朗的天气，你会有机会邂逅蝠鲼、鹰、海龟、公牛鲨、昆士兰巨型groper、梭鱼群、海蛇等等。

2. Barracuda Point

Barracuda Point is the best dive site of the World Famous Sipadan Islands. Here you could watch a huge shoal of thousands of Barracudas pass by underwater, making it a dream spot for underwater photography. Various reef Sharks and many pelagic fishes are also patrolling the area! Hammerhead sharks are a possible encounter too…

马来西亚沙巴的的西巴丹（Sipadan，诗巴丹）是马来西亚惟一的海洋岛屿，位于印度太平洋盆地的心脏，是世界上最富饶的海洋生物栖息地。这里有超过3000多种鱼类和成百上千种珊瑚。世界上罕见的潜水景观基本上都可以在诗芭丹见到：成群的梭鱼和大眼，玳瑁交配，以及一些深海物种，如蝠鲼（俗称魔鬼鱼）、鹰鳐、公子鲨和鲸鲨等。岛的正下方还有著名的海龟坟场，溶洞中通道错综复杂，里面随处可见因迷路而窒息的海龟尸骨。

： 1.码头悬壁（JETT DROP）

最大平均深度600公尺，是峭壁潜水之最，也是世界上最好的海滨潜水，而在最浅处则是夜潜的好去处。顺著峭壁往前行，海龟、鱼随时在你眼前穿梭而过，慵懒的来回觅食；还有其他多种鱼类如狮子鱼、娃娃鱼、海、独角鱼。晚上夜潜时，你可以看到身长1.5至2公尺的巨型铁头W哥在峭壁的洞穴中鼾眠。

2.海龟洞（TURTLE CAVE）　此潜点平均深度15～20公尺，最大深度21公尺。此潜点必须要导潜的陪伴，切忌单独潜水入龟洞；而适当且良好的浮力与耗气控制是潜入龟洞最基本的技巧，另外进入此龟洞时要注意蛙鞋的摆动，以免影响洞内的能见度。此地已成诗巴丹的名潜点，洞穴内有许多海龟的骨骸，增添些许神秘及惆之传奇。在龟洞的入口有一醒目的告示牌，警告潜水者不要冒然进入，如果要进入，岛潜的协助与潜水灯绳索都是必备的。

3.梭鱼坪（BARRACUDA POINT） 此点平均深度14公尺，最大深度600公尺。此潜点变化多端，物种极其丰富；扮演主角的上千b金梭鱼在此觅食，形成的梭鱼风暴场面极其壮观，是此地最让人震撼奇特的景观。而在最浅的沙地上，有许多的园及白，而天蝎鱼、石头鱼、叶鱼、色　鲜艳的裸鳃类、河豚、海龟、炮弹鱼在此也可看到。鱼在这里显得格外的亲切，或许是因槭澄锓岣唬使得们对於潜水者兴趣缺缺吧。此点水流较强劲，须特别注意。

4.珊瑚花园（CORAL GARDEN） 此点平均深度10公尺，珊瑚种类繁多是此点的特色，包括鹿角珊瑚、桌面珊瑚、各种　软珊瑚与桶状珊瑚。其他的海洋生物还有炮弹鱼、大石斑、龙王、海龟、豹等，这　豹身上的斑点极其特殊，而流线壮硕的体型绝对媲美一般白。

5.白之路（WHITE TIP AVENUE）　平均深度16公尺。此潜点有巨型的软硬珊瑚，尤其是黑珊瑚与巨型海扇及海绵。而大型的鱼群、鱼、海龟、Jack Fish、炮弹鱼是此地的常客，还有美丽且种类多的裸鳃类；这是个让人振奋的潜点。

6.中间点（MID REEF）　此地珊瑚及鱼类丰富，就犹如其他的潜点一样令人赏心悦目，且在此处潜水你可看到奇特的叶鱼。

7.海龟镇（TURTLE PATCH）　平均深度14公尺。虽说在诗巴丹潜水随时可以看到海龟，但是在这里你可看到更多的海龟，此点几乎就是海龟的栖息处，海龟在此到处漫游。另外这里的大型桌状珊瑚也比比皆是，色彩艳丽的天蝎鱼在桶状海绵里休息，而难得一见大型的炮弹鱼也可在5公尺的浅水处看到。

9.鹿角顶饰（STAGHORNCREST）　此点平均深度20公尺，也是梭鱼及Jack Fish的聚集地。洞穴中的龙虾是摄影者的最爱，你可以各种角度去抓取龙虾的神态；而此地的Jack Fish也非常的和善，你可加入它们的行列或在它们旋转的圆中心体验特殊的感受。

10.龙虾穴（LOBSTER LAIR）　顾名思义的是这里有槭不少的龙虾，梭鱼及Jack Fish在此也不会缺席；另外在此也有大型的桌面珊瑚及大型鱼的清洁站。

11.悬旎ㄔ埃HANGING GARDEN）　大型鱼类及海扇是此地的特色，软硬珊瑚的分岩卜浅Ｍ暾，还可仔细的观察一些小东西如裸鳃类、叶鱼、娃娃鱼等。

The World famous Richelieu Rock is located near the Surin Islands in Thailand. Regarded as one of the best spots to have a chance of spotting the gentle giant Whale Shark underwater, this spot has been described as a “Whale Shark magnet”! 斯米兰群岛位于泰国普吉岛西北方约85公里的安达曼海上，大小岛屿一共九个；每三个一组，分成三组排列。这一带水域的群岛早已被泰国政府列为国家海洋保护公园，且分别于第四岛，第八岛设有管理处，负责监管各群岛的活动。特别是泰国海军负责在各潜点及下锚区装设浮标以供船只悬系之用，从此不准再任意下锚，以免伤害珊瑚等生物。

This fascinating huge wreck dive site is a 130m long armed cargo ship which was hit by a Japanese torpedo during World War 2 and then pushed back into the water in 1963 by a volcano eruption! It is a very easy dive but with a whole lot of things to see such as Garden Eels, a nice swim-through inside the wreck and big schools of fishes. This spot offers spectacular opportunities for underwater photography so don’t forget your camera!

6. Cod Hole

Cod Hole is a very famous dive site of the Great Barrier Reef, much like North Horn. It is an easy reef dive where you can observe and also feed huge friendly Potato Groupers. There are also harmless Whitetip Reef Sharks and Napoleon fishes swimming about. The visibility is fantastic and the diving conditions are usually ideal making it the perfect place for underwater photography!

7. Navy Pier

This is a rather unusual dive site beneath an Australian Navy Pier in Exmouth in Western Australia. The conditions are not great here with poor visibility but the fish life is just astonishing with an incredible biodiversity in such a tiny place. Here you can see Nurse Sharks, the bizarre looking Wobbegong Shark and many strange small creatures such as Flat Worms, Nudibranchs, and Frog fishes. This makes it one of the best shore dives in the world and a real dream for any underwater macro photographer!

尊重不同文明 共建和谐世界

请相信，世界是平的！ 尊重不同文明 共建和谐世界

――温家宝总理在法国巴黎综合理工大学的演讲

（2005年12月6日，巴黎）

尊敬的教育部长先生，尊敬的校长先生，同学们，老师们：

　　法国巴黎综合理工大学是法兰西的骄傲。这里聚集了才华横溢的知识精英，培养出许多像贝克莱尔、勒威耶、阿莱这样的杰出人才，为法兰西乃至世界文明的进步做出了贡献。应邀来这所知名学府演讲，我感到很高兴。

我演讲的题目是：尊重不同文明，共建和谐世界。

在人类社会的历史长河中，我们勇敢、智慧和勤劳的祖先创造了丰富多彩的文明。随着时间的推移，这些文明有的成为了历史，有的生生不息地一直延续下来，有的相互交融产生了新的文明。今天，人类文明正在发生深刻的变革。科技进步和经济文化交往缩短了各种文明之间的距离。无论在巴黎的香榭丽舍大街还是在北京的长安街，都可以看到不同服装、不同肤色、不同母语的人们接踵而行。无论在东方还是西方，人们的交往从来没有像今天这样密切，影响人们日常生活的因素已不再局限于某一种文化。文化是一个民族的灵魂，是她赖以生存和延续的基础。无论对中华民族还是对法兰西民族来说，我们各自继承和发扬的文化都是民族之根、国家之魂。文化多样性是人类文明的重要特征。文化多样性之于人类社会，就如同生物多样性之于自然界一样，是一种客观现实。只有尊重文化的多样性，才能使人类文明得以发展。

如何才能使不同文明共存和发展，归根到底在于“和”。这就是国与国之间的和平，人与人之间的和睦，人与自然之间的和谐。

站在人类文明发展的高度上，我们应该把和平放在第一位。不同文明的国家之间有没有可能和平相处？答案是肯定的。我们生活的这个星球上，有60多亿人口，200多个国家，2500多个民族，6000多种语言，有基督教、天主教、伊斯兰教、佛教和道教等多种宗教。正是这些不同文明的相互依存、相互交流、相互借鉴、相映生辉，才构成今天这个丰富多彩的世界。中国自古就有以和为贵、和而不同、和实生物的思想。“以和为贵”就是说国家之间、民族之间、人与人之间要以团结互助、友好相处为最高境界；“和而不同”就是说一个国家、一个民族既能容纳不同的文明存在，又能保留自己的优秀文明传统；“和实生物”就是说只有不同文明之间相互吸收借鉴，才能文物化新，推进文明的进步。“和”是中国文化传统的基本精神，也是中华民族不懈追求的理想境界。早在一千多年前，中国的唐代对外交流就非常活跃。世界上与唐朝交往的国家有七十多个。丝绸之路上和平的使团、商队络绎不绝。中国文化那时就传播到了东罗马帝国、阿拉伯国家，同时唐代的舞蹈、音乐、绘画、食品、服装、宗教也吸纳了外来文化的精华，将中华文明推向一个新的高峰。

人与人之间的和睦相处是社会文明的重要标志，也是国家稳定的基础。中国古代著名思想家孟子说过：“天时不如地利，地利不如人和。”就是说只要人们和睦相处，就什么困难都能克服。要真正实现人与人之间的和睦，就需要发展社会生产力，消除贫穷与落后，使人们过上富裕的生活；就需要实现社会的公平与正义，坚持法律面前人人平等，尊重和保障人权；就需要提倡不同民族、不同信仰的人们相互包容、相互尊重、与人为善、以邻为伴。

人与自然的和谐相处是人类文明发展的前提。中国文化提倡“天人合一”的思想。所谓天人合一，包含着人与自然界相统一的意思。资源与环境是人类生存的基本条件，人类文明的发展从来就是依附于自然的。人可以认识自然，在与自然的和谐相处中谋生存、求发展，而不能破坏自然。有的古文明由兴盛走向衰败的一个重要原因就是对自然界肆意开发和掠夺，最终导致自然对人类的惩罚，酿成了文明的悲剧。因此，关爱自然，善待自然，是全人类的共同利益。一个失衡的地球是支撑不起现代文明大厦的。

刚刚过去的20世纪是人类文明大发展的时期。在这100年中，科技上的进步、经济上的发展、思想上的解放和艺术上的创新，都是人类智慧空前的展现，是以往几千年都难以做到的。然而，事情还有另外一面，20世纪同样见证了人类之间的相互残杀，对自然的大规模破坏和大量的贫困、饥荒、疾病。21世纪人类文明正面临前所未有的发展机遇，也面临空前的挑战。只有实现了国与国之间的和平，人与人之间的和睦，人与自然之间的和谐，人类文明才能持续发展。

世界上任何一种文明都是在变革中发展进步的。中国古代的哲学经典《周易》提出“穷则变，变则通，通则久”的思想。中华文明源远流长，却不是一成不变的。几千年来，中华文明延续发展，虽然在近代曾经一度落后，但又能奋起图强，大步前进，这不是偶然的。中华文明发展的基础和内在动力，在于它的刚健自强，在于它的独立意志，在于它的开放包容，在于它的维新变革。中华文明正是通过不断变革而传承下来并发扬光大的。

上个世纪中叶，新中国的成立，标志着“中国人从此站立起来了”。这是中华文明漫长历史中的一个重要里程碑。中国的社会主义社会是一个变革的社会，是一个开放的社会，是一个不断发展和完善的社会。改革开放将贯穿中国社会主义现代化建设的全过程。上世纪70年代末以来，中国在社会主义制度的基础上实行了改革开放的政策。中国的改革是全面的改革，我们在推进经济体制改革的同时，积极推进政治体制、文化体制和社会管理体制等方面的改革。实行改革开放，就是要充分激发亿万人民群众的积极性和创造性，进一步解放和发展生产力，不断满足人们日益增长的物质文化需要；就是要充分吸收和借鉴世界一切优秀文明成果，使社会主义永葆生机与活力；就是要贯彻科学发展观，构建和谐社会，实现人的全面发展；就是要健全民主制度，扩大公民有序的政治参与，贯彻依法治国的基本方略，建设社会主义法治国家。总之，我们通过改革将使社会更加发达、更加自由、更加平等，也更有秩序、更有法制，使中华文明更加灿烂辉煌。

摆脱贫困、谋求发展，是一代又一代中国人的追求和梦想。多少年来，我们的民族，即使在最艰难的时刻，心中总有着一盏明灯，它照亮我们的前程，使每个中国人燃起希望和勇气的火焰。我们深知，中国是一个拥有13亿人口的不发达国家，在发展中所遇到的问题，无论就其规模还是复杂性而言，都是举世罕见的，彻底摆脱贫困和落后还有很长的路要走。但我们坚信，一代又一代中国人传承着希望和勇气的灯火，不畏艰辛、百折不挠、团结奋斗，一定能够把中国建设成富强、民主、文明的社会主义现代化国家。

中国坚定不移地走和平发展道路，实行互利共赢的开放政策。全世界有识之士都看到，中国的发展对世界是机遇，而不是威胁。中国的稳定和发展本身就是对世界和平与繁荣的贡献。中华民族的悠久历史和深重灾难，培养了她自强不息、厚德载物的民族精神。中国作为世界大家庭中的一员，千百年来虽饱经忧患，但以自己的勤劳和智慧推动着世界文明的进步与发展。中华民族历来是一个讲信修睦、崇尚和平的民族。近代以来，中华民族曾经饱受列强入侵的苦难，深知和平的可贵。中国走和平发展道路是基于中国历史文化传统和现实利益需要的必然选择，是长期的、坚定不移的。

1805年，拿破仑一世将学校变为军校。拿破仑认为“给那些不是出身富裕家庭的孩子以先进的教育是危险的”，于是他停止了学校的免费制度，制定了高昂的年学费，并修改了入学考试的选拔制度，使得在那些付费的中学学习的经历成为入学必不可少的条件，而这些学校只有那些有产阶级的孩子才能上的起。的确如此，早年德国、日本的义务教育制度和普选制度是“煽动家”和“组织者”迅速窜红并夺取政权，导致灾难性后果。所以，英国的义务教育很晚才推出，贵族精英一直把控大局。1870年英国颁布《初等教育法》。该法第5条规定，每个学区都应在公立学校为本学区的所有儿童提供教育；第74条授权学校委员会制定地方法规，强制5至13岁儿童入学。

义务教育（Compulsory education）起源于德国。宗教领袖马丁・路德是最早提出义务教育概念的人。改革胜利后，为使人们都有学习《圣经》的能力，路德颁布了义务教育法。1619年，德国魏玛公国公布的学校法令规定：父母应送其6-12岁的子女人学，这是最早的义务教育。在1763年到1819年，德国基本完善了义务教育法规。

Chinese premier gives speech at France's Ecole Polytechnique 6 Dec.

BBC Monitoring International Reports

December 08, 2005

(From BBC Monitoring International Reports)

Chinese Premier Wen Jiabao has praised the long-standing friendship between China and France. In his speech at the Ecole Polytechnique he quoted Mencius and from the Book of Changes. He said Chinese philosophers have had a great impact on French thinkers in the 17th century and the ideals of the French Revolution provided an intellectual weapon for China's movement against feudalism and colonialism in the 20th century. Wen Jiabao also said China still has a long way to go before it can eradicate poverty and backwardness. He added it is not by chance that the Chinese civilization has continued to develop over thousands of years, "flourishing once again and moving forward with giant steps after being plunged into a state of backwardness for a time". Wen said the Chinese people will be able to build China into a modern socialist country that is prosperous, strong, democratic, and civilized and people of vision all over the world will see China's development as an opportunity, not as a threat. The following is the "full text" of the speech by Chinese Premier Wen Jiabao at Ecole Polytechnique in France on 6 December, entitled: "Respect different civilizations and jointly build a harmonious world", carried by official Chinese news agency Xinhua (New China News Agency)

Paris, 6 December: State Council Premier Wen Jiabao, who is currently on a visit here, delivered an important speech titled, "Respect different civilizations and jointly build a harmonious world", at Ecole Polytechnique in France on 6 December. The full text of the speech follows:

Respect different civilizations and jointly build a harmonious world

Speech by Premier Wen Jiabao at Ecole Polytechnique in Paris (6 December 05)

The Honourable Mr Minister of Education,

The Honourable Mr President,

Students and faculty members:

Ecole Polytechnique is the pride of France. Here the gifted and talented cream of the intelligentsia is brought together, and many outstanding figures such as Becquerel, Le Verrier, and Allais were trained here. This has contributed to the progress of French civilization and even the world's civilizations. I am greatly delighted to be invited to speak at this prestigious university.

My speech is titled: Respect different civilizations and jointly build a harmonious world. In the long span of the history of human society, our brave, intelligent, and diligent forebears created rich and colour[ful] civilizations. With the elapse of time, some of these civilizations became a thing of the past; some have continued to exist through ceaseless expansion; and some have become new civilizations through intermingling. Today human civilization is undergoing profound changes. Scientific and technological advances and economic and cultural exchanges have shortened the distance between various civilizations. Whether on Champs Elysees in Paris or Changan Avenue in Beijing, we see people of different skin colours dressed in different attire who speak different languages walking closely together. Whether in the East or the West, contact among people has never been closer, and factors that affect people's day-to-day lives are no longer confined to a certain culture. Culture is the soul of a nation, and it is the basis on which she depends on for her survival and continuation. For either the Chinese nation or the French nation, the culture that we have inherited and carried forward is the root of our nation and the soul of our country. Cultural diversity is an important feature of human civilization. It is an objective reality that cultural diversity is to human society as biodiversity is to the natural world. Only when cultural diversity is respected can human civilization be developed.

How to enable different civilizations to exist and develop side by side depends, in the final analysis, on "harmony." This means peace among states, amity among people, and harmony between man and nature.

As far as the development of human civilization is concerned, we should give top priority to peace. Can countries with different civilizations coexist in peace? The answer is in the affirmative. On this planet where we live, there are more than 6 billion people, over 200 countries, more than 2,500 ethnicities, and over 6,000 languages. There are many religions such as Christianity, Islam, Buddhism, and Daoism. It is the interdependence, interchange, mutual influence, and mutual enhancement of these different civilizations that constitute the varied and colourful world today. Since ancient times, China has espoused a kind of thinking that cherishes peace, allows for differences in harmony, and believes in the harmonious interplay of differences as a source of new things. "Cherishing peace" means that countries, nations, and people should take unity, mutual assistance, and friendly coexistence as their supreme ideal. "Differences in harmony" means that a country or a nation should accommodate the existence of different civilizations while retaining its own splendid tradition of civilization. "Harmonious interplay of differences as a source of new things" means that only if different civilizations draw upon each other will there be new cultural attainments and progress in civilization. "Harmony" is the basic spirit of China's cultural heritage; it is also an ideal that the Chinese nation has pursued tirelessly. More than 1,000 years ago, China's Tang dynasty was already engaged in extremely brisk diplomacy with other countries. More than 70 countries in the world engaged with the Tang dynasty. There was an endless stream of peace emissaries and trade caravans along the Silk Road. Chinese culture spread to the Eastern Roman Empire [the Byzantine Empire] and Arab countries. At the same time, the Tang dynasty also incorporated the finest elements of foreign cultures into its dance, music, paintings, food, dress, and religion, thereby taking Chinese civilization to a new high.

Coexistence in harmony among people is an important hallmark of civilization in society; it is also the foundation of national stability. Renowned ancient Chinese thinker Mencius said: "Climate and weather are not as important as geographical positions. Geographic positions are not as important as harmonious human relations." This is to say that as long as people live in harmony side by side, they can overcome any difficulties. To truly achieve harmony among people, we need to develop the productive forces in society, eradicate poverty and backwardness, and enable people to lead an affluent life. We need to achieve social equity and justice, uphold equality for all before the law, and respect and safeguard human rights. We need to encourage mutual tolerance and respect among peoples of different ethnicities and faiths, the cultivation of friendly ties with other peoples, and the treatment of one's neighbours as partners. The harmonious coexistence of man and nature is the premise for the development of human civilization. Chinese culture advocates the thinking that "man is an integral part of nature". "Man as an integral part of nature" implies the unity of man and the natural world. Resources and the environment are the basic conditions for the survival of humankind. The development of human civilization has always depended upon nature. Man can understand nature and survive and develop through living in harmony with nature; he cannot destroy nature. An important reason why some ancient civilizations went from flourishing to decline is because they wantonly exploited and plundered the natural world, ultimately leading to the punishment of humankind by nature and bringing about a tragedy of civilization. For this reason, taking good care of nature and treating nature kindly serves the common interests of all humankind. A globe out of balance cannot support the edifice of modern civilization.

The 20th century, which came to an end a short time ago, was a period of great development for human civilization. Scientific and technological advances, economic development, ideological emancipation, and artistic innovation in those 100 years displayed the wisdom of humankind as never before, something that had not been achieved in the several thousand years before that. However, there is another side to the story. The 20th century also witnessed mutual slaughter among humankind, the massive destruction of nature, and endemic poverty, famine, and disease. Human civilization in the 21st century is faced with opportunities for development as never before as well as unprecedented challenges. Human civilization can continue to develop only if peace among countries, amity among people, and harmony between man and nature are achieved.

All civilizations in the world experience development and progress in the course of changes. The Book of Changes, the ancient Chinese classic of philosophy, advanced the idea that "when things have run their course, change occurs; when change occurs, possibilities open up; when possibilities open up, things last". Despite its ancient roots, Chinese civilization is not immutable. It is not by chance that Chinese civilization has continued to develop through several thousand years, flourishing once again and moving forward with giant steps after being plunged into a state of backwardness for a time in the modern period. The foundation and inner drive for the development of Chinese civilization spring from its vigour, self-strengthening, independent will, openness, tolerance, and change. It is through constant changes that Chinese civilization has been inherited and carried forward.

The founding of New China in the middle of the last century signified that "the Chinese people would henceforth stand up". This was an important milestone in the lengthy history of Chinese civilization. The socialist society in China is a changing society, an open society, and a constantly developing and improving society. Reform and opening up will run through the entire process of China's socialist modernization. Since the end of the 1970s, China has implemented the policy of reform and opening up on the basis of the socialist system. The reform that China pursues is a comprehensive one. At the same time that we are pressing forward with economic structural reform, we are actively pushing forward structural reforms in politics, culture, and social management. To reform and open up is to fully inspire the initiative and creativity of millions upon millions of people, to further liberate and develop the productive forces, and to constantly meet people's growing material and cultural needs. It means fully assimilating and drawing upon all outstanding achievements of civilization in the world in order to maintain the vitality and vigour of socialism. It means implementing a scientific development concept, building a harmonious society, and achieving the all-round development of man. It means establishing a sound democratic system, expanding citizens' orderly participation in politics, implementing the basic strategy of running the country by law, and building a socialist country under the rule of law. In short, we are working for greater development, greater freedom, greater equality, greater order, and more legal institutions in our society through reform so as to bring greater splendour to Chinese civilization.

Escaping poverty and pursuing development has been the ideal and dream of successive generations of Chinese. Through the ages, a bright light always shone in our nation, even at its most difficult moment. It illuminated our future and stoked the flames of hope and courage in every Chinese. We know full well that China is an underdeveloped country with a population of 1.3 billion, and the problems it has encountered in the course of development have been rarely seen in the world in terms of their scale and complexity. We still have a long way to go before we can eradicate poverty and backwardness. Nevertheless, we firmly believe that the Chinese people will be able to build China into a modern socialist country that is prosperous, strong, democratic, and civilized by passing the torch of hope and courage from generation to generation and by working in unity with an indomitable spirit without fear of hardship.

China steadfastly follows the path of peaceful development and implements a mutually beneficial and win-win policy of opening up. People of vision all over the world see China's development as an opportunity for the world, not as a threat to it. China's stability and development itself contribute to world peace and prosperity. The long history of the Chinese nation and the intense suffering it has endured has fostered a national spirit characterized by ceaseless self-strengthening and by a great virtue that sustains all things. As a member of the community of countries in the world, China has, despite having endured untold suffering through the millennia, has promoted the progress and development of the world's civilizations through its diligence and intelligence. In modern times, the Chinese nation has suffered greatly from the aggression of foreign powers, and it has come to understand the value of peace deeply. The path of peaceful development is an inevitable choice that China has made in light of its historical and cultural traditions and practical interests, and one that China will follow unswervingly over the long term.

Ladies and gentlemen:

Cultural exchanges between China and France have been widely acclaimed in the history of the development of Eastern and Western civilizations. The Chinese people have long shown a keen interest in French culture. Books by such thinkers as Rousseau and Montesquieu have long been translated into Chinese and widely read by progressive Chinese intellectuals. The ideal of "freedom, equality, and fraternity" in the French Revolution spread to China and provided an intellectual weapon for China's movement against feudalism and colonialism in modern times. Chinese thinker Yan Fu propounded the view that "The most valuable thing to a person is freedom and the most valuable thing to a country is independence." In modern times, many Chinese revolutionaries, thinkers, writers, and artists went to France to study and were influenced by French culture. Zhou Enlai and Deng Xiaoping, Chinese leaders of the older generation, studied and worked in France, where they pursued new knowledge.

Beginning in the 17th century, Chinese Confucian classics such as The Analects and The Great Learning were introduced to Europe through France. Paris was once the hub of "Chinese cultural fervour" in Europe. A number of outstanding French thinkers such as Descartes, Voltaire, Quesnay, and Montesquieu were well-versed in Chinese culture. In his Essay on the Manners and Spirit of Nations, Voltaire wrote: "China has a long history that cannot be matched by any other country in the world, and it has fostered a glorious rational culture. While the other nations were inventing allegorical fables, the Chinese had started writing their history." Many contemporary French statesmen and writers have a deep affinity for Chinese culture. French poet Saint-John Perse, winner of the 1960 Nobel Prize for Literature, finished his masterpiece Anabase in a Daoist temple in the western suburb of Beijing.

Sino-French relations are now going through the best period in history, with ever-stronger mutual trust in the strategic arena, increasingly closer economic and trade relations, and unprecedented brisk cultural exchanges. The Chinese government sets great store by the friendly and cooperative relationship with France, and the Chinese people cherish their friendship with the French people. We view France as a trustworthy friend and partner. We have full confidence in the prospect of Sino-French relations.

Ladies and gentlemen:

Culture is the best bridge that connects people's souls. French civilization and Chinese civilization are both the wonders of the world's civilizations, and both have deep historical roots and exude boundless vitality in the course of innovation. We hope that the just-concluded Chinese Culture Year and French Culture Year will serve as a new historical starting point for cultural exchanges between China and France. Your school motto is "For the Nation, Science, and Glory"; it represents the French youth's ideal and goal. Youth is the hope of a country and the future of the world. I earnestly hope that the Chinese and French peoples, especially young people from the two countries, will join hands to strengthen exchanges, enhance understanding, add lustre to Chinese and French civilizations, and jointly build a new world of peace, amity, and harmony.
